Teaching Dance at FCSN

Jasmine has been volunteering for many years with Friends of Children with Special Needs (FCSN), a Bay Area nonprofit dedicated to enriching the lives of children and young adults with developmental disabilities. As a volunteer dance instructor, she teaches dance, music, and other creative activities to kids with special needs — bringing joy, confidence, and a sense of rhythm into their lives through movement.

AI Generated Dance Project

Inspired by her years teaching dance at FCSN, Jasmine created a set of AI-generated dance clips designed specifically for young learners and kids with special needs. The clips use EDGE (Editable Dance GEneration), a generative AI model from Stanford University that choreographs realistic, physically plausible dance animations synchronized to any piece of music. (Stanford HAI, April 2023)

The key advantage of EDGE is its ability to analyze a song's rhythm and emotional tone and generate choreography that a real dancer could actually perform — no frame-by-frame animation required. For this project, Jasmine chose kid-friendly, upbeat music — tracks kids already know and love — so the AI-generated moves are energetic, playful, and easy to follow along with. This makes learning to dance more fun and less intimidating, especially for children who benefit from visual, movement-based instruction.

The raw clips were then post-processed in 3D software to polish the lighting, colors, and character look, giving the animations a bright, appealing finish that captures kids' attention and keeps them engaged.
